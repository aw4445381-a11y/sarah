Today’s Devotionals

Small reflections to shepherd the heart back to peace.

🌅

Morning — Gentle Beginnings

Start with a whispered “Here I am, Lord.” Breathe slow. Read Matthew 6:25–34. Let the lilies teach you how to live without hurry.

  • Read: Matthew 6:25–34
  • Prayer: “Jesus, quiet my anxious mind.”
  • Practice: 5 minutes of silence with slow breathing.
🌤️

Midday — Bread in the Middle

In the middle of the day, invite God into the ordinary: emails, dishes, conversations. Whisper thanks. Read Luke 24:13–35 and notice how Jesus meets us on the road.

  • Read: Luke 24:13–35
  • Prayer: “Open my eyes to Your nearness.”
  • Practice: Gratitude list (3 items).
🌙

Evening — Rest that Heals

Lay the day down. Confess what weighed heavy. Read Psalm 121. Remember who keeps watch — the One who does not sleep.

  • Read: Psalm 121
  • Prayer: “Keep me in Your peace.”
  • Practice: Light a candle, 10 deep breaths.

The Journey with Jesus

Milestones and little mercies I don’t want to forget.

  1. The First Yes

    I said “yes” to Jesus at a youth camp; everything felt like sunrise. (Romans 10:9)

  2. Learning to Rest

    Burnout taught me Sabbath: cease, delight, rest, worship. (Matthew 11:28–29)

  3. Hospitality as Worship

    Opened our home; found God in soup pots and unhurried tables. (Hebrews 13:2)

  4. Prayer in the Ordinary

    Prayer walks, laundry prayers, bus-stop prayers — a liturgy of the everyday. (1 Thessalonians 5:17)

Daily Practices

Simple rhythms that pull life toward wholeness.

🕊️

Sabbath

One day to stop, delight, rest, and worship. Phones away; souls awake.

  • Start with a blessing.
  • Cook something slow.
  • Take a sunset walk.
📖

Scripture

Read slowly; underline the verbs; ask, “What does this reveal about God?”

  • Lectio: Read • Reflect • Respond • Rest.
  • Journal one sentence.
🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Community

Text one friend to encourage them. Pray their name out loud.

  • Practice listening first.
  • Bring bread; stay present.
🕯️

Prayer

Short honest prayers, often. God delights in your voice.

  • Morning Examen: Gratitude • Grace • Go.

FAQs

What translation do you read?

I rotate between ESV and NIV. I also enjoy listening to an audio Bible while walking.

How long do your devotionals take?

5–15 minutes. A little goes a long way when done often and honestly.

How do I start praying?

Be simple and specific. “Jesus, here’s where I’m scared. Help me.” He hears you and loves you.
